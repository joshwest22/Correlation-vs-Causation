Correlation vs Causation

Correlation implies that there is a relationship between variables. Correlation only applies when the relationship is linear.

The measure for correlation is represented by the correlation coefficient, r, which ranges from -1 to 1. The close r is to 1 or -1, the stronger the relationship is to a varible.

It is crucial to recognize that correlation is NOT the same thing as causation. That is to say that just because data lines with a trendline doesn't mean that one variable caused the effect represented by the data.

According to Cairo, the only reasonable way to determine causation is to run randomized controlled experiments until all irrelevant variables have been eliminated.
